Gulf Shores

Gulf Shores is a suburban town located on the Gulf Coast of Alabama. It is the southernmost city and a popular tourist attraction. The city was not incorporated until 1958. The completion of the Intercoastal Canal, brought growth and development to the area and by Summer of 1960, the town had become tourist centered, having multiple hotels and motels for Summer crowds. The permanent resident population in Gulf shores is 15,014 and the median income is $51,862, which is well below the $59,910 median of the state. In 2022, the city introduced a $15 million revitalization plan that will focus on sustainable and low impact strategies.

Fun Facts

At 112 feet tall, the Wharf Ferris Wheel is the second tallest in the Southeast

Admiral Farragut famously said, “Damn the torpedoes…full speed ahead,” while off the shore of Fort Morgan, during the Battle of Mobile Bay in 1864.

Its believed the first Native American village in the United States to have been visited by an explorer was at the location of Shellbanks Baptist Church in Gulf Shores.

Gulf Shores actually has four incredible ecosystems including swamp, freshwater marsh, longleaf pine savanna, and ancient maritime oak.
