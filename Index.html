Alabama

Alabama is a state in the southeastern part of the United States, where it borders the Gulf of Mexico. Alabama is nicknamed the Yellowhammer state, after it's state bird. It is also known as the "Cotton State" and the "Heart of Dixie." Alabama is the 30th largest state by area and 24th by population. It's population is 5,024,279, which equates to about 94 people per square mile.Alabama's main demographic is White, at 66.8%, followed by Black at 26.5%. English is by far the most spoken language in the state at 96.1%, followed by 2.2% Spanish. Alabama is located in the middle of the "Bible Belt," and about 86% of the population identifies as part of the Christian faith The state has invested heavily in various industries such as aerospace and vehicle manufacturing. Which is a sharp turn from the agricultural facing economy of the last century Alabama is also home to the "Crimson Tide," of the University of Alabama, which is known for it's multiple SEC and NCAAF championships over the last decade. It is not uncommon for residents of Alabama to greet each other with the phrase "Roll Tide," showing support for one of the preeminent college football teams in the country.

Fun Facts

Windshield wipers were invented in Alabama.

Alabama was the first state to recognize Christmas as a holiday.

In 1968, the first 911 call was made in Haleyville, Alabama

Alabama was home of the Saturn V rocket, which took the first men to the moon.

When most people hear Mardi Gras, they think about New Orleans, but Mobile, Alabama was home the the original celebration in 1703.
