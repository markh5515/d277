Huntsville

Hunstville is an urban city in north central Alabama. It is located along the Tennesse River in Madison County. Huntsville was founded in what was the Mississippi Territory, and became incorporated in 1819. It was originally the capital, but after a year, it was decided to move the capital to a more central location. Huntsville has seen significant growth since World War II, thanks to the establishment of Redstone Arsenal. The city is home to the U.S. Space and Rocket Center, and is known as the Rocket City. In 2021 Huntsville became the largest city in Alabama. It has a population of almost 222,000 people. The median household income is $67,784, which is significantly higher than the median income of the rest of the state, which is $59,910. These high wages stem from the various military contracts in the area held by companies such as Lockheed Martin, Raytheon and Boeing. This presence has created an influx of high end tech jobs for the city.

Fun Facts

There are more PhD's here per capita and is considered one of the country's most educated city

Lowe Mill, located in downtown Huntsville, is one of the nation's largest artist colony.

In 1967, Huntsville hosted the first ever launch of the Saturn IV rocket.

Huntsville is home to the country's largest annual yard sale, spanning 36.6-miles.
